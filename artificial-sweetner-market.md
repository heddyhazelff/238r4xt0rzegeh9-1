<p><p>The artificial sweetener market includes various types such as Aspartame, Acesulfame-K, Monosodium Glutamate (MSG), Saccharin, and Sodium Benzoate. Aspartame is widely used in low-calorie products due to its high sweetness intensity. Acesulfame-K is often combined with other sweeteners for enhanced flavor. MSG, while primarily a flavor enhancer, is categorized similarly in food products. Saccharin, one of the oldest sweeteners, offers a zero-calorie option. Sodium Benzoate acts as a preservative, complementing sweeteners in processed foods. These products cater to health-conscious consumers seeking alternatives to sugar.</p></p>
